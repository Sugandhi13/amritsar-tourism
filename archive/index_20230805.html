Amritsar - The Holy City

Why Amritsar is a must visit place?

About City

Amritsar is founded in the fifteenth century by Guru Ram Das Ji, the fourth guru of the Sikhs. The heritage city of Amritsar is one of the most spiritually significant and historically rich cities of India.

Rich History

Amritsar has witnessed one of the significant moments of India’s independence at the Jallianwala Bagh. On 13th April 1919, hundreds of Indian civilians were killed on the orders of British Colonel Reginald Edward Harry Dyer.

Relegious Significance

Sikh’s prime place of worship, Golden Temple, is in Amritsar. It is the Sikhs holiest gurudwara. Go on a spiritual tour and feel blissful at the gurdwara, primarily known as Harmandir Sahib.

Dynamic Culture

Amritsar is the largest city of Punjab. The chief festivals celebrated at Amritsar comprise Vaisakhi, Lohri, Guru Parb and Holi. Bhangra and Gidda are the special dance forms performed by the folks of Amritsar.

Tasty Food

A visit to Amritsar is incomplete without tasting the authentic cuisines of Punjab. Try Amritsari kulcha and lassi when in the city. Also, sarso ka saag tastes different in Punjab than it does in the rest of the country.

Shopping

There are a lot of options for shopping in Amritsar that represent rich handicraft culture of Punjab like Phulkari dupattas, Salwar Kamiz, Punjabi Jutti to traditional carpets etc.
